Providing Guidance

Guidance is your opportunity to give the vendors context to your questions. Providing this guidance is crucial to enabling these vendors to give you the most relevant responses possible. Your guidance should including the following:

  1. 1. Stage and timing — Provide context as to where you are in a purchasing process. Examples below:

    • “We’re just first trying to gain an understanding of what this category is. As such,we do not have a formal budget, process or timeline in place as of yet. However,if we do uncover that this could drive significant value, we would absolutely take steps to go down a more deliberate purchase evaluation process”
    • “We are highly interested in this category as we believe we can derive significant value from implemented such a solution. That being said, we are at the beginning stages of our evaluation process. We will likely make a purchasing decision in the next 6-9 months.”
    • “We are likely to make a purchase in the next 3-6 months and are ensuring we are aware of all vendors in this space and narrowing it down to a short-list”
  2. 2. Your knowledge level — Help the vendor understand your knowledge level so they know whether they should focus on introducing you to the most basic concepts, should jump to the most intricate and cutting edge details or somewhere in between.
  3. 3. Existing solution/provider — The more a vendor knows about what you have in placetoday, the more they can highlight what they perceive to be the benefit they can bring above and beyond what you are doing today.
  4. 4. Known constraints — Anything else that a vendor should know that would help them self select themselves in/out of a dialogue with you will increase the chances of onlyspeaking with vendors who conceivably could be a fit.
