FilmFreeway Re-design

The challenge is to re-design the existing FilmFreeway homepage. It's important to mention that the website is good-looking, modern, and responsive. I tried to stick to the elements and pages you have, so the changes won't have a global impact. Here are my thoughts and suggestions on how we can improve the design and UX.

Things To Consider

  • The first screen may be challenging to understand what the service is about.
  • Some blocks copy one another. Seven blocks with 9 CTA's lead to 3 pages.
  • Some blocks are not informative enough and look generic (title, 1 sentance, CTA, background photo).
  • Red, green, blue and white colors for interactive elements.

Possible Ways of Improvements

  • Make sections more informative (like the one with the festival grid), including more informative headings.
  • Reduce the total length of the page by removing the duplicating blocks, but store even larger amounts of information.
  • Explain users on the go why and how they can use FilmFreeway on the first screen (for creatives and festivals).
  • Add some visual diversity to the sections, bring consistency to the style (reduce the amount of colors for interactive elements).

Old & New Versions

First Screen Matters

As your services is made for 2 user groups: filmmakers and film festivals, I suggest we should propose for every group a guide through the service from the very first glance.

There are two tabs to guide the user: one for a creative and the other one is for a festival holder.

I kept all the CTA's but re-grouped them. New users will be taught how to use the website, and members will be able to submit their entries or start listings on the go.

I used placeholders as icons right now. I'll design them in the next iteration.

More Personal & Friendly Content

Showing real people's tweets as testimonials would be a more efficient way to tell about your service.

Unlike impersonal quotes without links and photos, tweets are more personal and frienldy, and more creadible source of information.

Some Extra Tips

  • It will be nice te reverse the timeline on the Testimonials page. That way users will be able to explore the most recent comments first.
  • Based on the most common searches, we can add quick filters to the Browse Festivals page. The filters like 'this week', 'this month', or simply the most popular tags, like 'LA' or 'indie'.
