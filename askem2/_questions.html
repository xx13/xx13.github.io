Asking Good Questions

Ask ‘Em is designed help you get enough of an understanding about a vendor to make an informed decision as to whether it makes sense to take a call or meeting with them. As a director at a Fortune 50 CPG company once said: “I use Ask ‘Em to be able to gain sufficient enough awareness to: 1. understand what the vendor does, 2. understand how we might use them and 3. what key information I need to learn next to find out if we should prioritize engaging with them further.” Here are some good examples which you are free to use.

  • Please provide an overview of your service? What does it do?
  • Please provide an overview of your tech? How does your service do what it does?
  • What does it typically take to get up and running with your service? What inputs are required from us? What types of resources are typically required? How long does it typically take to implement?
  • What KPIs/metrics would a company like ours typically focus on improving by using your product?
  • Where/how would we measure your product’s impact on those KPIs?
  • Who do you compete with?
  • What is the biggest differentiation/advantage you have over your competitors?
  • Do you have any case studies that you can share with us?
  • Do you typically work with customers in our industry (e.g. Financial Services)?
  • What is an example of a company who is clearly very successful implementing a solution such as the one you provide - even if they are not your client? Help us see what we should be striving to achieve and surpass.
  • What is most basic, immediate use-case that a company like ours might pursue with your solution?
  • Once we get through a basic use-case, where might we go from there? What’s the more advanced user organization doing with your solution?
  • What question haven’t we asked you? How would you answer it?
  • Finally, what’s the vision look like? Describe what we might be doing/achieving in 3 years?
  • What is the most common misconception people have about your solution or yourcategory?
  • Do you integrate with X, Y or Z?
  • Do you have any key partnerships we should know about?
  • Can you describe your pricing model?
